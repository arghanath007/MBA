* **Since** -> A beginning at a point of time in the past and continuing to the present. [MEANING] [VERY IMPORTAN]
* **For** -> Duration/Time period. [MEANING] [VERY IMPORTAN]
* I have lived in mumbai **for** 7 years -> Correct. [Example] 
* I lived in mumbai **for** 7 years -> Correct. [Example]
* **By** -> Certain time period in the future. [MEANING] [VERY IMPORTAN]
* I will reach mumbai **by** tomorrow evening. [Example]
* You will have finish writing CAT by the end of november this year. [Example]

* Interjection -> an ejaculatory utterance usually lacking grammatical connection -> a word or phrase used in exclamation (such as Heavens! Dear me!) -> a cry or inarticulate utterance (such as Alas! ouch! phooey! ugh!) expressing an emotion. [MEANING]
* The verb is the most important part of speech. [VERY IMPORTANT]
* If you understand the verb correctly, then 50% of grammar is done. [VERY IMPORTANT]

* While -> At the time of. [Usage 1]
* While -> Contrast. [Usage 2]
* While I am good in VARC, my QA is very poor. [While -> To show contrast]
* **Beside VS Besides**. 
* **Beside** -> Position/Preposition. [MEANING] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* **Besides** -> Adverb -> Adverbial Conjunction -> Adding something. [MEANING] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* She sat beside me. [Correct] [Example]
* Adverbial Conjunction:- [VERY IMPORTANT]
    * Add -> Moreover, besides, also, even.
    * Contrast -> However, nevertheless, yet.
    * Consequence -> Consequently, accordingly.
    * Conclusion -> Hence, therefore, thus, ...

* If there is a verb(finite) then there has to be a subject accompying the verb -> DNA of a sentence. [VERY IMPORTANT]
* Delhi is the capital of India. [Example]
* Finite -> is, non-finite -> No. 
* I want to sleep. [Example]
* Finite -> want, non-finite -> to sleep.
* to + verb -> infinitive. [VERY IMPORTANT]
* non-finite -> It means that it will never change. [VERY IMPORTANT]
* Swimming -> Swimming within itself is an activity. [VERY IMPORTANT]
* Swimming is my hobby. [Correct] [Example]

* Subject(S) + Verb(V) + Object(O) -> Active Voice. [FORMULA] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* I -> 1st person.
* She -> 3rd person.
* I, She -> Singular.
* She wants a glass of water. [Example] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* They want a glass of water. [Example] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* They -> Plural.
* They -> 3rd person.
* Active -> Subject(S) + Verb(V) + Object(O). [FORMULA] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* Passive -> Object(O) + Verb(V) + Subject(S). [FORMULA] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* The verb changes from active voice to passive voice. [FORMULA] [VERY IMPORTANT]
* I love katrina Kaif. [Example] [Active Voice]
* Katrina Kaif is loved by me. [Example] [Passive Voice]
* I hurt my brother. [Example] [Active Voice]
* My brother has been hurt by me. [Example] [Passive Voice]
* Yesterday I hurt my brother. [Example] [Active Voice] [Past tense]
* My brother was hurt by me the day before. [Example] [Passive Voice]

* In the middle of the night -> No meaning/makes no sense -> Phrase. [Example]
* In the middle of the night, I woke up -> Makes sense -> Not a phrase. [Example]
